Population Per Square Mile in Iowa School Districts

Population per square mile in the state of Iowa. Clusters occur in the metropolitan areas of the state as to be expected. Des moines, Iowa City, and Davenport contain the highest populations within school districts. The western portion of the state has much lower population densities especially in the southern regions which tend to be more rural. Data obtained from the Iowa Geospatial Data Clearinghouse.

Number of Same Sex Households in Iowa

This map visualizes the prevalence of same sex couple households in the state of Iowa using graduated symbols and a sequential color scheme. Same sex couple households are more prevalent in the eastern portion of Iowa. More urban counties such as Polk County and Johnson county have higher amounts of same sex couple households. Northwestern Iowa has lower rates of same sex households which is most likely due to the area being more rural and having more conservative values. The data was gathered from the National Historical Geographical Information System.

Median Household Income and Percent African American or Black Individuals Occupying Counties of Alabama

Map visualizing the relationship between median household income and percent of African American or Black individuals within Alabama counties. Southern counties in Alabma have highly concentrated areas where African American and Black populations dominate while having a much lower median household income. There are very few coutnies where there are heavy populations of African America/Black people that have relatively high median househole income. Areas with higher median income in the northern portion of the state also contain lower percentages of African American/Black populations. This trend occurs throughout the southern United Staes due to historical racial injustice and residential segregation. The data for this map was gathered from The National Historical Geographical System using census data from 2020-2024.

Flow of Bananas Within South America in 2019

Flow map visualizing the flow of bananas in and out of South American countries in 2019. Ecuador appeared to have the most outward transport of bananas with a total outflow of 495,549 bananas. Argentina and Chile had the highest total amount of bananas due to their high amounts of imported bananas. Data was represented using flow lines for banana flows, graduated symbols for total flow, and a sequential color scheme for net flow of bananas.
